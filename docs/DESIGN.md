## Problem Statement

Build an open-source system where a compound-engineering agent loop owns the full
lifecycle of a disciplined day-trading *research* operation — proposing strategies,
backtesting them honestly, grading them, allocating across them, and running them
in continuous paper trading — entirely in public. The human is the "genesis prompt
and observer": they interact through GitHub issues, sessions, and prompts, but do
not write code, design models, grade success, or keep the agent on track. No real
money is used; success is an honest, reproducible, out-of-sample paper track record
shown in public across daily / weekly / monthly horizons and across market regimes.

Every strategy fits one of two shapes:
- **Overnight:** buy at close, sell at next open (long or short)
- **Intraday:** buy at open, sell at same-day close (long or short)

Universe (eventual target): S&P 500 and Russell 2000 constituents, plus ETFs listed on
AMEX / NASDAQ / NYSE. **Phase 1 deliberately starts with liquid ETFs only** (clean,
survivorship-free data); single-name coverage is added once point-in-time membership and
borrow assumptions exist — see Phasing and Known Biases. Each strategy carries a
**regime gate** (e.g. SPY > 200DMA, or 200DMA sloping up) that switches it on/off based
on market conditions.

## What Makes This Cool

- **The constraint collapses the hard part.** Because every trade is close→open or
  open→close, a trade's P&L is fully determined by daily open and close prices. No
  intraday/tick data is ever required. The entire backtest is a vectorized matrix of
  overnight/intraday returns masked by a regime signal. Free EOD data is sufficient,
  and the core engine is hundreds of lines, not a framework port.
- **Honesty is the product.** The gross overnight edge is largely eaten by
  transaction costs at the index level and has decayed since ~2015 (one SPY study:
  +717% before costs, −32% after, 1993–2020). So the rare, credible, valuable public
  artifact is not a wall of green — it is a system that transparently shows what does
  NOT survive costs and out-of-sample testing, and only promotes the few things that
  do. "Sometimes the best trade is no trade" stops being a slogan and becomes the
  literal baseline: **cash is the defending champion.**
- **The agent owns the loop.** The most novel piece is the meta-layer: a public repo
  where md files, skills, prompts, context, and a GitHub-native automation loop let an
  agent build, run, grade, and maintain a research operation with the human only
  seeding intent and observing. The track record is the proof that the loop works.

## Constraints

- **Capital model:** fixed **$10,000 per day, non-compounding.** Each day starts from
  the same notional, so per-period skill is isolated and a few lucky early compounding
  wins cannot dominate the record. **Budget semantics (resolved in spec review):** the
  **overnight book** and the **intraday book** are each evaluated as a *separate* $10K
  book, so the two categories never compete for capital and can be compared cleanly.
  Within a book, the $10K is split across that day's selected names. Capital is **not**
  reused across boundaries within a day — each book is independent.
- **Cash baseline:** staying in cash is always a valid allocation, and the cash
  baseline **earns the risk-free / broker-sweep yield, not 0%** — in a higher-rate
  environment cash is a real hurdle a strategy must clear after costs.
- **Trade cap (resolved):** at most **10 entry positions per boundary per book per
  day** (up to 10 overnight entries and up to 10 intraday entries), customizable.
  Automatic exits at the resolving boundary do not count against the cap.
- **Decision timing:** **overnight (close-side) entries lock before the close auction;
  intraday (open-side) entries lock before the open.** Signals may use only data
  available *strictly before* the entry boundary (see look-ahead rules in Engine).
- **Holds are strictly single-boundary:** close→open or open→close. Never multi-day.
- **Universe liquidity floor:** screen out illiquid names where bid/ask and slippage
  make any "edge" an illusion. (Added during office hours — the cost research makes
  this non-optional.)
- **Data:** free, public EOD sources only.
- **Money:** none. Paper / simulated execution only.
- **Public:** public GitHub repo, Pages enabled, all work shown — issues and PRs are
  the audit trail.
- **Human role:** genesis prompt + observer. The agent does the building, designing,
  grading, and self-correction.

## Premises

These are the load-bearing assumptions, validated by research during the session.

1. **The deliverable is a credible, public, reproducible research process — not a
   money bot.** Credibility comes from honest, cost- and overfitting-adjusted
   reporting. (Confirmed.)
2. **Doing nothing leaves an idea and an empty repo.** The real artifact is proof that
   an autonomous compound-engineering loop can run a disciplined research operation in
   public. (Confirmed.)
3. **Daily OHLC is the backbone — no intraday data.** Data sufficiency holds fully.
   Revised: **transaction costs are decisive**, the gross overnight edge is largely
   cost-killed at the index level and decayed post-2015, surviving mainly in
   conditional / higher-volatility pockets. "No edge after costs" is an expected,
   valuable result, not a failure. **Scope note (spec review):** daily OHLC is
   sufficient for *idealized single-boundary return calculation*, not for full
   execution realism (auction fills, borrow, spreads). Results carry explicit
   known-bias labels — see "Known Biases & Data Realism."
4. **Distribution = the living public system,** not a downloadable artifact. A
   scheduled loop runs each market day, updates results, publishes to Pages, and uses
   issues/PRs as the audit trail. (Confirmed.)
5. **Don't reinvent.** Concrete reuse, behind a **data-provider abstraction** so no
   single source is load-bearing: Stooq (primary EOD *if automated access verifies*) +
   yfinance (fallback) with data-quality diff checks; optionally vectorbt (fast
   candidate sweeps); **mlfinlab if it installs cleanly, otherwise hand-rolled** DSR /
   PBO / purged-CV primitives (a few hundred lines); MABWiser / PyPortfolioOpt
   (allocation); hmmlearn (regime detection); Alpaca paper (optional execution).
   (Confirmed.)
6. **Rigor is day-one scope or the board is noise.** Named methods: explicit
   per-trade cost + slippage model; **purged walk-forward cross-validation with
   embargo** (López de Prado); **Deflated Sharpe Ratio + Probability of Backtest
   Overfitting via CSCV** computed against an **immutable trial ledger** that counts
   *every* generated, mutated, and rejected variant — not just promoted ones — so the
   multiple-testing penalty is honest. (Strengthened.)
7. **Adaptive allocation is a first-class component (the meta-strategy).** Online
   allocation over the strategy pool — multi-armed bandits (EXP3 / Thompson, built for
   non-stationary markets) or online-portfolio methods (Hedge / AdaHedge) — conditioned
   on detected regime (HMM or rule-based). It answers "which of 100 strategies do I
   take seriously today, and which depend on the current market?" The meta-strategy is
   itself a competitor that must beat cash and naive baselines after costs. (New, added
   by the user during the session.)

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Thin vectorized core, GitHub-native
  Summary: Custom ~few-hundred-line engine (pandas/numpy). Strategies as small
           YAML/Python specs. Vectorized return matrix → P&L with explicit cost model.
           Scorer = DSR + standard metrics. Allocation = regime-gated equal-weight +
           cash. Static dashboard to Pages. GitHub Actions cron runs EOD. Paper
           execution deferred.
  Effort:  S
  Risk:    Low
  Pros:    Fastest to a live public track record; minimal dependencies; the daily-OHLC
           eureka makes the core tiny and fully legible (every number auditable in
           public); cost model front-and-center.
  Cons:    Reinvents some wheel; no realistic intra-bar fill modeling (fine for EOD);
           bandit/meta-strategy and paper execution are later phases.
  Reuses:  Stooq, pandas/numpy, GitHub Actions, gstack/compound-engineering loop.

### Approach B: Framework-anchored research stack
  Summary: Anchor on vectorbt (throughput for sweeps), mlfinlab (DSR/PBO/purged CV),
           MABWiser + PyPortfolioOpt (allocation), hmmlearn (regime), Alpaca paper from
           day one. Richer dashboard build.
  Effort:  L
  Risk:    Med
  Pros:    Best long-term rigor and throughput; battle-tested overfitting controls;
           realistic paper execution early; meta-strategy is first-class immediately.
  Cons:    Heavier dependencies, more setup, more surface for the agent to get stuck
           maintaining; some libs less event-realistic (fine for EOD); framework
           lock-in risk.
  Reuses:  vectorbt, mlfinlab, PyPortfolioOpt, MABWiser, hmmlearn, Alpaca, Stooq.

### Approach C: Falsification engine / tournament-first (chosen, built on A)
  Summary: Reframe the system's *job* as killing strategies, not collecting winners.
           Every strategy runs a gauntlet: cost model → purged walk-forward →
           DSR/PBO gate → regime-conditioned paper tournament at fixed $10K/day. Cash
           is the defending champion; nothing earns a leaderboard slot until it beats
           cash after costs out-of-sample. A public "graveyard" of killed strategies is
           a first-class output. The adaptive-allocation meta-strategy is just another
           contender that must win its way in. Built on A's lean stack, borrowing B's
           specific rigor libraries (mlfinlab) where they earn their keep.
  Effort:  M
  Risk:    Low–Med
  Pros:    Turns the transaction-cost reality from a threat into the product's reason to
           exist; maximizes public credibility (showing what does NOT work is rare and
           valuable); encodes "no trade is a valid trade" as the literal baseline; gives
           the compound-engineering loop a crisp objective (try to kill; promote
           survivors).
  Cons:    Philosophically demanding; slower to show "wins"; needs the rigor stack
           present early (mitigated by starting on A's lean foundation).
  Reuses:  A's stack + mlfinlab (DSR/PBO/purged CV); later hmmlearn, MABWiser, Alpaca.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach C implemented on an A foundation.** Lean, GitHub-native, organized as a
falsification/promotion tournament. Component map:

1. **Data layer.** A **provider abstraction** (Stooq if automated access verifies,
   yfinance fallback, data-quality diff checks) — no single source is load-bearing.
   Adjusted OHLC for the universe with a liquidity floor (e.g. dollar-volume threshold).
   Local cache (parquet) so backtests are reproducible and offline-capable. A
   point-in-time universe membership table to avoid survivorship bias (Phase 1 sidesteps
   this by starting with liquid ETFs).

2. **Strategy library.** Each strategy is a small declarative spec (YAML + optional
   Python hook): `category` (overnight | intraday), `side` (long | short),
   `universe`/`selection` (how it ranks/picks names each day), `regime_gate`
   (boolean market condition), and `params`. The library stores, versions, and
   "dreams up" strategies (agent-proposed parametrizations and new selection rules).

3. **Engine.** Vectorized daily-OHLC backtester. Computes per-name overnight and
   intraday returns, applies regime mask + selection + side, applies the **explicit
   cost + slippage model**, enforces the trade cap and single-boundary holds, and
   produces cumulative P&L and a full trade ledger.
   - **Look-ahead rules (resolved):** overnight (close-side) entries may use only data
     available *before* today's close; intraday (open-side) entries may use only data
     through the *prior* close. When in doubt, lag the signal one day. Regime gates
     (e.g. `SPY > 200DMA`) are evaluated on data strictly prior to the entry boundary.
   - **Corporate actions / bad data:** use split- and dividend-adjusted OHLC; for
     overnight holds, dividends accrue to longs and are charged to shorts on the
     ex-date gap; skip names with a missing open or close; treat halts/delistings as a
     forced exit at the last valid price. Maintain a **point-in-time universe
     membership** table to avoid survivorship bias (see Known Biases for the free-data
     limitation).
   - **Fill realism:** do **not** assume the exact official open/close is achievable.
     Apply a conservative slippage model (fixed bps + a liquidity-scaled term) on top
     of commissions, exclude names below the liquidity floor, and publish a
     **sensitivity table across cost assumptions** with every result.
   - The *same* spec drives realtime paper execution (optional, later phase) — backtest
     and live share one code path.

4. **Scorer / falsification gate.** No strategy is "good" until it survives, in order:
   (a) positive net of costs, (b) purged walk-forward out-of-sample with embargo,
   (c) **Deflated Sharpe + PBO/CSCV** penalty computed against the immutable trial
   ledger, (d) beats the **cash baseline (at risk-free yield)** after costs. Only
   survivors are promoted to the **leaderboard**; everything pre-gauntlet lives on a
   clearly-labeled **candidate board** (ungraded), and failures go to a public
   **graveyard** with the reason they died. The **trial ledger** assigns every
   attempted strategy/parameter set a unique immutable id; mutations and retries
   register as new trials and count against the multiple-testing budget.

5. **Adaptive allocation (meta-strategy).** Online allocation across *promoted*
   strategies — bandit (EXP3/Thompson) or online-portfolio (Hedge/AdaHedge) — gated by
   a regime detector (rule-based first, HMM later). Allocates each fixed **$10K/day,
   non-compounding** book, with cash (at risk-free yield) as a first-class arm, and
   respects the per-boundary trade cap and decision cutoffs. **Default production
   allocator is equal-weight + cash; the learned meta-strategy runs in shadow mode and
   only takes command once it beats that benchmark and cash after costs out-of-sample.**

6. **Dashboard.** Static site → GitHub Pages: the graded **leaderboard** (DSR-ranked),
   the ungraded **candidate board**, the **graveyard**, today's intended trades,
   realized daily/weekly/monthly P&L vs the risk-free cash baseline, regime state, the
   cost-sensitivity table, and per-strategy detail. Generated by the post-close job; no
   server.

7. **Meta-loop (compound engineering).** The repo's md files, skills, prompts, context
   docs, and a set of GitHub Actions workflows. **Three schedules, not one (resolved):**
   a **pre-open** job locks intraday open-side entries and resolves the prior overnight
   book; a **pre-close** job locks overnight close-side entries; a **post-close (EOD)**
   job ingests official OHLC, runs the gauntlet on candidates, updates allocation,
   simulates the day, regenerates the dashboard, commits, and opens/closes issues and
   PRs as the audit trail. **Operational spec:** deterministic steps run as plain Python
   in CI; agent/LLM steps (proposing and triaging strategies) run as bounded,
   budget-capped invocations; broker/API credentials live in GitHub Secrets; any job
   failure opens an issue and the loop is idempotent so it can re-run safely. A
   **data-provider abstraction** sits behind Stooq with a yfinance fallback and a
   data-quality diff check, so a blocked or changed source degrades gracefully instead
   of corrupting results. The human seeds intent and observes via issues; the agent runs
   everything else.

**Phasing:**
- **Phase 1 (skeleton):** data-provider abstraction (Stooq + yfinance fallback) +
  parquet cache + vectorized daily-OHLC engine + explicit cost model + risk-free cash
  baseline + one reference strategy per side + static dashboard + the three Actions
  schedules. **Start the universe with liquid ETFs only** (clean data, no survivorship
  problem) and label any single-name results as exploratory/biased until point-in-time
  membership exists. Phase 1 publishes a **candidate board**, not a leaderboard.
- **Phase 2 (rigor):** trial ledger + purged walk-forward CV + DSR + PBO/CSCV gate +
  graveyard + cost-sensitivity tables. Only now does the **leaderboard** exist.
- **Phase 3 (allocation):** regime detection + bandit/online-portfolio meta-strategy in
  **shadow mode** (equal-weight + cash is the production allocator until the learned
  one earns command) + fixed-notional daily simulation. Expand to single names once
  point-in-time membership and borrow assumptions are in place.
- **Phase 4 (realtime paper, optional):** Alpaca paper execution sharing the engine's
  code path; realtime daily tracking vs backtest expectation. Treated as optional
  validation, not core architecture.

## Resolved Decisions (from spec review)

- **Short realism is a gate, not an option.** Shorts are restricted to liquid ETFs /
  large caps until a borrow-fee + hard-to-borrow model exists; no short strategy is
  promoted without it.
- **Cash baseline = risk-free / broker-sweep yield**, not 0%.
- **"Leaderboard" is reserved for post-gauntlet survivors.** Pre-gauntlet results are a
  labeled "candidate board." This keeps the Issue #1 constitution ("nothing reaches the
  leaderboard until it beats cash after costs out-of-sample") internally consistent.
- **Three schedules** (pre-open, pre-close, post-close), not a single EOD cron.
- **Trade cap = 10 entries per boundary per book per day**; capital is two independent
  $10K books (overnight, intraday).
- **Promotion is not permanent.** Promoted strategies are re-evaluated each cycle and
  **demoted to the graveyard** when the growing trial ledger erodes their DSR/PBO
  standing or their out-of-sample / live performance decays below the cash baseline.

## Known Biases & Data Realism

Stated plainly so the public track record is honest about its own limits:

- **Free EOD data does not include** reliable point-in-time index membership, delisted
  names, borrow availability/fees, or bid/ask spreads. Until these are sourced, claims
  are scoped to *idealized single-boundary return calculation*, and biased results are
  labeled as such on the dashboard.
- **Official open/close prices are not guaranteed fills.** Conservative slippage and a
  liquidity floor partly compensate; the cost-sensitivity table shows how conclusions
  move as assumptions tighten.
- **Stooq access can change or be blocked.** The provider abstraction + yfinance
  fallback + data-quality diff checks contain this risk.
- **Compute limits.** Russell 2000 × many years × many variants is batched, uses
  cached return matrices and float32 where acceptable, and is bounded by per-run trial
  budgets to fit GitHub Actions.

## Open Questions

- **Cost model calibration.** What exact commission + spread + slippage assumptions are
  "honest" for this universe? (Proposal: conservative fixed bps per side + a
  spread/slippage term scaled by each name's liquidity; publish the assumptions and the
  sensitivity table.)
- **Regime taxonomy.** Start rule-based (200DMA level/slope, volatility buckets) and
  graduate to HMM later, or commit to HMM sooner?
- **`mlfinlab` vs hand-rolled rigor.** Verify `mlfinlab` installs cleanly from PyPI; if
  not, implement DSR, PBO/CSCV, and purged CV directly (they are a few hundred lines)
  rather than depend on a possibly-stale package.
- **Pages build tool.** Plain generated HTML vs a static framework (Quarto / Astro).
- **Language.** Python is assumed (quant ecosystem). Confirm no preference otherwise.

## Success Criteria

- The loop runs unattended each market day and publishes updated, reproducible results
  to GitHub Pages without human code changes.
- Every reported number is reproducible from cached EOD data and committed code.
- The leaderboard reports cost-adjusted, DSR/PBO-penalized, out-of-sample metrics and
  ranks every strategy against the cash baseline.
- A visible graveyard shows killed strategies and why — the system is willing to say
  "no edge."
- Continuous daily/weekly/monthly paper P&L is tracked vs cash across at least one full
  regime change, with the meta-strategy beating naive equal-weight and cash after costs.

## Distribution Plan

This is a continuously running public system, not a downloadable artifact.
- **Hosting:** GitHub repo (public) + GitHub Pages for the dashboard.
- **CI/CD:** GitHub Actions — **three scheduled workflows** (pre-open, pre-close,
  post-close) behind the data-provider abstraction. The post-close job pulls data, runs
  the gauntlet, updates allocation, simulates the day, regenerates the dashboard, and
  commits; the pre-open and pre-close jobs lock that boundary's entries.
  PRs/issues are the audit trail and the human interface.
- **Reproducibility:** pinned dependencies, cached EOD data committed or rebuildable,
  and deterministic backtests so any observer can re-run and verify.
